### **Simple explanation**
SEBI is India’s stock market regulator. It just told companies that create big stock or bond indices — like Nifty 50 or CRISIL bond indices — that if their indices are “significant,” they must register with SEBI within 6 months. “Significant” means lots of mutual fund money, over ₹20,000 crore, is following that index. The idea is to make sure these index makers have proper rules, transparency, and a way for investors to complain if something goes wrong.

### **The core idea**
1. **Big indices need oversight**: If a mutual fund index has >₹20,000 crore tracking it, the company making that index must register with SEBI.
2. **More accountability, less risk**: Registration brings governance standards and a formal grievance redressal system for investors.

### **Key concepts**
- **1. What’s a ‘significant index’**: Daily average cumulative AUM tracking it across MF schemes > ₹20,000 crore for each of the previous 6 months ending June 30 and Dec 31.
- **2. 6-month deadline**: Current index providers must apply for registration within 6 months. They can keep operating while SEBI processes it.
- **3. 48 indices identified**: Based on July–Dec 2025 data, SEBI found 48 such indices from NSE Indices, BSE Index Services, CRISIL.
- **4. RBI exemption**: Indices notified by RBI as ‘significant benchmarks’ or ‘authorised benchmarks’ are exempt from SEBI registration.
- **5. How to lose ‘significant’ status**: If AUM stays below ₹20,000 crore for 3 straight years or six consecutive half-yearly reviews.
- **6. Separate entity rule**: If a SEBI-registered firm runs index business “departmentally,” it must create a separate legal entity within 2 years.
- **7. Grievance redressal**: Only applies to SEBI-registered ‘significant indices’. Investors in those can complain under Index Provider Regulations.

### **One analogy**
Think of indices like **textbooks for mutual funds**. If a textbook is used by classes with >₹20,000 crore of “student fees” riding on it, SEBI wants the publisher registered, following quality standards, and with a complaint cell. Small textbooks don’t need this.

### **Common confusions**
1. **“All indices need registration” → No**
   Only ‘significant indices’ with >₹20,000 crore AUM. Small or niche indices are not covered.

2. **“RBI indices also need SEBI nod” → No**
   If RBI already notifies an index as a ‘significant’ or ‘authorised’ benchmark, SEBI rules don’t apply to that provider.

3. **“Index providers must stop working now” → No**
   They can continue if they file the application within 6 months.

# Slide 1 — SEBI Directs ‘Significant Indices’ to Register

### What Happened?

- Securities and Exchange Board of India directed providers of ‘significant indices’ to register within six months
    
- Registration will take place under the **Index Provider Regulations**
    
- RBI-notified ‘significant benchmarks’ and ‘authorised benchmarks’ are exempt
    
- An index qualifies as ‘significant’ based on mutual fund assets tracking it
    
- The rule applies if average cumulative AUM exceeds ₹20,000 crore for six months
    

---

# Slide 2 — Why It Matters

### Why This Is Important

- Strengthens transparency and accountability in index management
    
- Improves regulatory oversight of widely tracked market indices
    
- Protects investors relying on benchmark-linked mutual funds
    

### Key Numbers

- Registration deadline: **6 months**
    
- AUM threshold: **₹20,000 crore**
    
- Measurement dates: **June 30 and December 31**
    

### Simple Definitions

- **Index:** Group measuring market performance
    
- **Benchmark:** Standard used to compare investments
    
- **AUM (Assets Under Management):** Total money managed by funds
    
- **Significant Index:** Widely tracked investment index
